A century-old butcher on Arima's main lane frying Kobe-beef croquettes to order — the onsen town's best 400-yen lunch, eaten standing with a hot can of tea.

## Details
Usually 10:00–17:00, sold out early on weekends. The minchi-katsu (minced cutlet) outranks the plain croquette; get one of each.

## Insider notes
Two doors down, the sansho-pepper tsukudani shop gives samples — the classic Arima souvenir locals actually buy. Do this circuit between baths.
