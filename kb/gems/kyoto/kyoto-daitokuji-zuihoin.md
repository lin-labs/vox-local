While the crowds fight over Ryōan-ji's rock garden, this sub-temple of Daitoku-ji has an equally stunning raked-gravel "sea" by the same 20th-century master, Mirei Shigemori — and you'll often have it entirely to yourself.

## Details
09:00–17:00, ¥400. Part of the Daitoku-ji complex; combine with Kōtō-in (maple corridor) if it's open.

## Insider notes
The hidden crucifix stone arrangement in the rear garden nods to the founding daimyo's Christian conversion — ask the monk at the entrance, some enjoy telling the story. Best light before 10:00.
